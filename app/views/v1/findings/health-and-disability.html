Health and disability

Who’s in this group?

You’re disabled under the Equality Act 2010 if you have a physical or mental impairment that has a ‘substantial’ and ‘long-term’ negative effect on your ability to do normal daily activities. Research used to inform this review includes participants from within and outside the UK. As such, evidence is likely to relate to a slightly more opaque group of individuals.

What affects this group’s participation in the labour market

  • Issues regarding an individuals health condition
  • Lack of job opportunities
  • Insufficient skills, experience or qualifications
  • Difficulty with transport
  • Attitudes of employers
  • Difficulties using facilities or equipment
  • Anxiety, lack of confidence or motivation
  • Costs associated with employers making adjustments
  • Caring responsibilities
  • Financial situation
